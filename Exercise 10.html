TWICE

A South Korean girl group formed by JYP Entertainment through the 2015 reality show Sixteen. The group debuted on October 20, 2015 with the extended play The Story Begins.

MEMBERS

  • Nayeon | Lead vocalist, lead dancer, center
    • - The oldest member of Twice, One of the strongest vocalists in the group, Nayeon frequently starts Twice’s songs and delivers the chorus measures.

  • Jeongyeon | TWICE Lead vocalist
    • -Trained for five years before launching her idol career as a vocalist in Twice. She is referred to as the group’s embodiment of the “girl crush.”

  • Momo | TWICE Main dancer, sub vocalist, sub rapper
    • - Hailing from Japan, Momo is not only viewed as the best dancer in Twice but one of the best dancers across all female K-pop acts.

  • Sana | TWICE Sub Vocalist
    • - Another member from Japan, is a vocalist with an infectiously bright personality.

  • Jihyo | TWICE Leader, main vocalist
    • - The leader of the group, Jihyo became a K-pop idol trainee in elementary school and trained for 10 years before debuting in Twice. She is the group’s main vocalist, with key lines in the choruses of Twice’s tracks

  • Mina | TWICE Main Dancer, sub vocalist
    • - The only member of Twice to be born in the U.S., Mina moved to Japan as a child. Besides being a singer, she is one of the group’s main dancers

  • Dahyun | TWICE Lead rapper, sub vocalist
    • - One of the main mood-makers of the group, Dahyun is an expressive rapper with punchy lines.

  • Chaeyoung | TWICE Main rapper, sub vocalist
    • - Since Twice’s debut, Chaeyoung has taken the role of the group’s main rapper, adding a fierce and playful energy to her verses. She is also an avid artist who has drawn the cover art for one of Twice’s limited edition albums

  • Tzuyu | TWICE Lead dancer, sub vocalist, visual
    • - Born in Taiwan, Tzuyu is Twice’s “maknae”—a term used for the youngest member in K-pop groups—and was only 16 at the time of the group’s debut.

HISTORY

TWICE is undeniably one of the most popular K-pop groups in South Korea, if not the most popular girl group. Formed by MNET’s survival show SIXTEEN, TWICE got a lot of attention during their debut at the end of 2015. But they exploded in popularity a mere six months later when the instant hit “Cheer Up” skyrocketed them to the top – and they’ve been there ever since. As the representatives of bubblegum pop, TWICE has delivered classic after classic such as “TT,” “Likey,” “What is Love,” “Fancy,” and many more. With catchy songs, fun dance moves, and a team of stunningly gorgeous ladies, it’s no wonder TWICE is as beloved as they are.

How was Twice formed

Twice originated from a reality competition show. The 2015 series Sixteen involved that many JYP Entertainment “trainees”—the term used for aspiring idols preparing for a potential debut—fighting for a place in the group’s final lineup. After a series of challenges testing the contestants’ singing, rapping and dancing abilities as well as charisma, seven individuals were eliminated, with the remaining nine forming a new ensemble.

Why Twice is the group name

How the group got the name "Twice", JYP Entertainment’s CEO Park Jin Young explained in a presscon: “It means that they will be able to move people twice. Once through the ears and once through the eyes.”

Twice fanbase name

Twice also has a name for its most dedicated fans—a feature shared by all K-pop acts (BTS’ fans are called “ARMYs,” Blackpink’s are “Blinks”). A follower of the girl group is a “Once.” The members explained the meaning on social media in 2015: “If you love us even once, we will repay your love with twice of our love.”

TWICE ACHIEVEMENTS

2016

  • TWICE ranked #14 on Tumblr’s 2016 list of Top K-pop Groups.

2017

  • TWICE was #18 on Billboard‘s list of “21 Under 21.”
  • TWICE became the first K-pop girl group to simultaneously top two of Billboard’s world charts: Twicetagram placed #1 on the World Albums chart, and “Likey” placed #1 on the World Digital Songs Sales chart. They are the second K-pop group to do this after BTS.
  • “Knock Knock” and “Signal” were the #1 and #2 most watched music videos in Korea on YouTube for 2017. They placed #4 and #6 respectively on the list for global Youtube views.
  • TWICE ranked #9 on Tumblr’s 2017 list of Top K-pop Groups.

2018

  • TWICE was one of six groups mentioned in TIME‘s list of Best K-Pop Artists.
  • “What is Love?” and “Dance the Night Away” were the #4 and #7 most watched music videos in Korea on YouTube for 2018. They placed #7 and #10 respectively on the list for global Youtube views (and “Heart Shaker” was #6).
  • TWICE ranked #11 on Tumblr’s 2018 list of Top K-pop Groups.
  • TWICE placed #33 on Billboard‘s Social 50 Year End Chart.

2019

  • TWICE ranked #11 on Tumblr’s 2019 list of Top K-pop Groups.
  • TWICE placed #3 on Spotify’s Top K-pop Artists of 2019.
  • TWICE placed on the following Billboard Year End Charts of 2019:
    • #10 on the World Digital Song Sales Artists Chart
    #16 on the Social 50 Chart
  • “Fancy” was the #3 most watched music video in Korea on YouTube for 2019.
  • Bugs Music named “Fancy” and “Yes or Yes” as the #5 and #6 Most Loved Music Videos on their 2019 Year End List.
  • “Fancy” and “Yes or Yes” placed #32 and #46 respectively on Genie Music’s Top Songs of 2019.

2020

  • TWICE was selected for Forbes‘s 30 Under 30 Asia List.
  • TWICE won “Best in Music” at the 12th annual Shorty Awards, which focuses on social media content.
  • TWICE made their debut on the Billboard 200 Chart with More & More (at #200).
  • “I Can’t Stop Me” topped Billboard‘s World Digital Song Sales Chart.
